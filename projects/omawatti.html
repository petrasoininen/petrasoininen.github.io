Team

I worked with designers Melissa Airaksinen, Ville Forsberg and Veli Jokela

My role

The UI audit and overall design process were carried out collaboratively as a team. I was primarily responsible for the views for customer information and billing.

This project was part of my studies at the Design Institute of LAB University of Applied Sciences.

Goal of the project

The goal of the project was to redesign Lahti Energia’s OmaWatti platform where customers can monitor their energy usage and electric bills. We examined existing services using a UI audit and heuristic evaluation to identify the main pain points. Based on the research, our goal was to align the visual identity of the OmaWatti service to align with Lahti Energia's website, clarify functionalities and consumption tracking, and improve accessibility.

View final prototype

UI audit

We examined OmaWatti through a UI audit and accessibility analysis. The evaluation was based on Nielsen’s 10 usability heuristics.

Based on the evaluation, the main areas for improvement were:

1

Clarify functionalities, consumption tracking and overall how information is presented.

2

Designing the service with a mobile-first and accessibility-focused approach.

3

Align the visual identity with Lahti Energia’s website.

Key takeaways

It was interesting to get the opportunity to redesign an existing service with a large customer base. The service included many different features and a lot of information to present, which made it challenging to display everything as clearly as possible. As my first client project, it was a very educational experience.
