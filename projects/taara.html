Problem

Sitting for long periods increases the risk of musculoskeletal disorders. The most effective way to prevent these issues is by taking regular and active breaks. Even light exercise with an exercise stick improves blood circulation and refreshes the mind.

The challenge is: How can we encourage employees to momentarily pause their work and take an active break that enhances well-being and mental clarity?

Solution

The Taara app is designed to serve as an exercise library and a reminder to take breaks during the workday. Users can log their completed exercises and track their duration. Gamified features encourage movement and enhance user engagement.

Take active breaks and browse exercise library.

Customize workout schedule to fit your workdays.

Earn rewards that motivate you to keep exercising.

User research

We conducted user research through a survey and user interviews.

The goal of the research was to understand office workers' interest and preferences regarding break exercises, as well as to identify motivational factors and potential app features.

Survey



The survey was conducted using Google Forms and it was open for approximately one and a half weeks. It was designed as a semi-structured questionnaire. A total of 34 responses were collected, with participants recruited through Facebook community groups


Themes of the survey: Demographic information and respondents' physical activity habits Features of the app Motivation for break exercises
The most popular features of the app were pre-set workouts categorized by duration. Personalization and reminders were also highlighted as important.

We explored motivation for break exercises through an open-ended question. The responses were grouped into six main themes: time personal health and well-being exercising together relieving muscle tension reminders other factors
Support and encouragement from employers were seen as necessary and even essential. In general, the benefits of break exercises for personal health and maintaining energy levels were seen as motivating. Additionally, the sense of community emerged as a motivating factor, with exercising together with colleagues being particularly encouraging.

User Interviews



The interviews were conducted remotely as open interviews. The results were compiled using an Affinity diagram.


Background Information: Five women, aged 26–60 Office workers, primarily screen-based jobs Varied physical activity habits
Additional Notes: Meal and/or coffee breaks are the only breaks during the workday.

Design drivers

Design drivers were defined based on user research.

1

Clear and Intuitive

Clear navigation provides quick, intuitive access without overwhelming the user.

2

Data Analysis and Tracking

Research showed interest in reviewing data regularly and fostering community through friendly competitions.

3

Motivation Through Gamification

Gamified elements encourage users to stay engaged and take regular active breaks.

4

Easily Followable Videos

Engaging, easy-to-follow videos help users perform movements correctly and stay motivated.

User flow

From our research, we developed user personas, scenarios, a story map and user flows to gain a deeper understanding of users’ goals and how they interact with the app.

First wireframes and navigation

Based on our background research, we started creating wireframes and exploring the overall structure of the app. At this stage, the visual style and potential gamified features were still rough ideas that evolved through multiple iterations.

Iterating wireframes

The wireframes went through several rounds of iteration, during which the visual style began to take shape. For user testing, we created a high-fidelity prototype to test the app with target users.

Usability testing

Five people participated in the testing.

During the test, the users completed four tasks related to different features:
Evaluating the clarity of navigation Setting a reminder Assessing the clarity of the exercise library Using the video view with the Taara exercise stick General usability and visual design
In addition to the tasks, the test users also filled out a background information form and prototype evaluation forms.

The test sessions were recorded in the school facilities using two cameras, and written notes were also taken during the testing.

Based on the test results, we focused on the following improvements: Added a shortcut to the user's own team in the profile Increased white space between elements in all views Enlarged clickable elements and some menu sizes Moved the exercise library filter to a floating element at the bottom of the page for better visibility Adjusted label colors and darkened the previously used light pink

Final product

A clear and structured home screen that immediately shows the user’s weekly progress, the next scheduled workout, and provides access to an alternative recommended workout and wellbeing articles.

A well-organized exercise library offering a variety of workout types and guided training videos designed for different goals and needs.

Encouraging challenges and the ability to create teams add a gamified element, maintain motivation, and strengthen the sense of community.

Managing profile and adjusting reminders to match individual work schedules enable a personalized app experience.

Tracking progress and earning rewards bring a gamified element to exercise, helping maintain motivation over time.
